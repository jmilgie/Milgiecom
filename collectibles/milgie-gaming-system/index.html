About the Artifact

Curatorial Statement

The Milgie Gaming System™ (2022) presents a domestic gaming platform as a formalized display object: operational, preserved, and staged with exhibition-first intent. Familiar electronics are recontextualized through deliberate enclosure, elevated supports, branded crestwork, and archival foam layout.

The primary unit is housed within a dedicated presentation case designed to read as a complete exhibit when opened. In parallel, a separate companion presentation box preserves two additional N64 controllers as a discrete controller archive—segregating control hardware from the primary unit to reinforce modularity, scalability, and museum-adjacent storage logic.

Printed ephemera and documentation are included as program materials. The system is presented without irony.

This notice affirms that the enclosed items collectively constitute the Milgie Gaming System™ (2022), a complete and intentional multi-box assembly consisting of: (1) the primary integrated display unit and its presentation case, (2) a separate companion controller archive box containing additional N64 controllers, and (3) associated printed ephemera and documentation.

All components were assembled as a unified work and presented as issued. Minor variations in finish, alignment, or foam tolerances constitute evidence of authenticity and should not be interpreted as defects.
