The Flying Saucers Chart

Originally produced July 2, 1956, this chart was created by Fred Keziah (1920–2010) — a Navy-trained illustrator who specialized in technical information visualization.

A comprehensive single-sheet reference compiling 228 illustrated UFO shapes, flight formations, caricatures of "space people," and tables of observed colors, sounds, speeds, and sizes.

Self-published in Arlington, Virginia in 1957 with a revised edition in 1958, sold by mail order for $1.00 per copy — an early landmark of data visualization applied to the unexplained.

The Concept of Visual Metaphysics
📡

Data Visualization Pioneer

Keziah's work was essentially an early form of data visualization applied to UFO reports — a methodical, almost scientific approach treating it much like a military recognition chart.

🛸

Post-War UFO Culture

In the post-war era of the 1950s, public fascination with space-age mysteries and esoteric spirituality converged. Keziah applied rigorous data design to these usually sensationalized topics.

🔭

Eclectic Inquiry

UFOs for No. 1, Reincarnation for No. 2 — Keziah's series reflects an era when the boundary between scientific inquiry and metaphysics remained delightfully blurred.
