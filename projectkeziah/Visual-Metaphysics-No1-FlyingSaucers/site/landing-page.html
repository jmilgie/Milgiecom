The Flying Saucers Chart

Originally produced in July 1956, "Visual Metaphysics No. 1: Flying Saucers" was created by Fred Keziah (1920–2010), a Navy-trained illustrator who specialized in technical information visualization.

This groundbreaking chart compiles 228 illustrated UFO shapes, observed formations and flight maneuvers, caricatures of "space people," and tables of reported characteristics—colors, sounds, speeds, and sizes—in a single comprehensive reference.

Self-published in Arlington, Virginia in 1957 (revised 1958), and sold by mail order for $1.00 per copy.

The Concept of Visual Metaphysics
📡

Data Visualization Pioneer

Fred Keziah's work was essentially an early form of data visualization applied to UFO reports—a methodical, almost scientific approach treating it much like a military recognition chart.

🛸

Post-War UFO Culture

In the post-war era of the 1950s, public fascination with both space-age mysteries and esoteric spirituality was on the rise. Keziah's unique contribution was applying data-driven rigor to these usually sensationalized topics.

🔭

Eclectic Inquiry

The series' eclectic range—UFOs for No. 1, Reincarnation for No. 2 (May 1957)—reflects the milieu of an era when the boundary between scientific inquiry and metaphysics remained delightfully blurred.
