The Reincarnation Chart

Originally produced May 28, 1957, this chart was created by Fred Keziah (1920–2010) — a Navy-trained illustrator who specialized in technical information visualization.

The second in Keziah's Visual Metaphysics series, this chart applied the same rigorous data-design approach as No. 1 — but to the cyclical journey of the soul. Geometric shapes, symbolic color theory, and spatial relationships communicate the eternal cycle of death and rebirth.

Self-published in Arlington, Virginia and registered with the U.S. Copyright Office in 1957. The chart disappeared from public view for decades until archival research uncovered references to its existence, inspiring this ongoing digital recreation project.

The Journey of Rediscovery
May 28, 1957

Original chart "Visual Metaphysics No. 2: Reincarnation" created by Fred Keziah and registered with the U.S. Copyright Office.

2021

Archival research uncovers descriptions and a partial photograph of the original via eBay search.

2022

Digital Recreation project begins, aiming to faithfully reproduce the visuals of the original work.

2025

Recreation site launched to document the ongoing journey and attract contributors with reference materials.

The Concept of Visual Metaphysics
♾️

Cycles of Existence

Keziah applied the same rigorous data-visualization approach he used for UFOs to the spiritual concept of reincarnation — mapping the cyclical journey of the soul through geometric precision and symbolic color theory.

🌀

Eastern Meets Western

The chart draws from Eastern philosophical traditions while employing the clean modernist aesthetic of 1950s American design — an era when the boundary between science and metaphysics remained delightfully blurred.

📡

Eclectic Inquiry

UFOs for No. 1, Reincarnation for No. 2 — Keziah's series reflects the milieu of the 1950s, when public fascination with both space-age mysteries and esoteric spirituality converged in popular culture.
