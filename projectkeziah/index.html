About the Project

Fred S. Keziah & the Visual Metaphysics Series

Fred S. Keziah (1920–2010) was a Navy-trained illustrator and animator who specialized in educational graphics and informational art. After his military service, he self-published a series of "information sheets" from his home in Arlington, Virginia.

These works occupy a unique intersection of mid-century design, information visualization, and spiritual exploration — bridging the precision of modernist design with the audacious subject matter of the 1950s counterculture fringe.

The Visual Metaphysics Digital Recreation Project was initiated by Johnathan Milgie in 2023, using high-resolution photographs of original poster editions. Reference images were provided by Richard Geldreich Jr. and Adam Gorightly.

📡

Data Visualization Pioneer

Keziah treated UFO reports like a military recognition chart — a methodical, almost scientific classification system decades ahead of its time.

🛸

Post-War UFO Culture

Published in the peak years of American UFO fascination, these charts document a cultural moment when the line between science and the unknown was delightfully blurred.

🔭

Preserved for the Public

After decades of obscurity, this meticulous digital recreation makes these rare artifacts accessible to researchers, enthusiasts, and the curious.
